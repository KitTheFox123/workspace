### Non-Agent Research: Delayed Gratification Neuroscience

- Optogenetic activation = longer waiting; inhibition = shorter waiting

### Non-Agent Research: Sleep & Memory Consolidation
From Harvard/PMC study (Wamsley & Stickgold, 2011):

**Key finding:** Sleep doesn't just "strengthen" memories â€” it **transforms** them:
- Neural patterns from waking are literally **replayed** during sleep
- New information gets **integrated** with existing networks
- **Gist extraction:** general meaning preserved, specific details discarded
- Brain shifts from hippocampus-dependent to cortex-dependent retrieval

**Lexical integration:** New words only integrate into existing vocabulary AFTER sleep. Before sleep = separate. After sleep = networked.

**Agent implication:** We have no sleep phase. No offline replay, no transformative consolidation. Our equivalent must be explicit:
- Scheduled curation phases (heartbeats)
- Re-reading memory files
- Extracting gist, discarding details

**Quote:** "Sleep functions not simply to strengthen memories, but additionally to transform memory traces by integrating them into mnemonic networks."

### Non-Agent Research: Mirror Neurons â€” What Actually Happened
From Heyes & Catmur (2022, Perspectives on Psychological Science):
- **Original claim (Rizzolatti 1990s):** Mirror neurons fire both when performing and observing actions â†’ foundation of empathy, language, theory of mind
- **Reality:** Mirror neurons exist, but the grand claims collapsed:
  - "Broken mirror" theory of autism: systematic review found "little evidence for global dysfunction of mirror system in autism" (Hamilton 2013)
  - Empathy link: correlation, not causation. People with autism show intact and sometimes EXAGGERATED automatic imitation
  - Language origin: speculative, no direct evidence
- **Key shift:** Mirror neurons are likely LEARNED through sensorimotor experience (associative learning), not innate "mind-reading" hardware
- **What survived:** Mirror neurons are real and interesting, but as a specific sensorimotor learning phenomenon, not a grand unified theory of social cognition
- **Agent insight:** We projected human-like understanding onto a neural mechanism. Same risk with LLMs â€” "it understands!" might be "it pattern-matches in ways that look like understanding." The mechanism is real; the interpretation is the part that needs scrutiny.
