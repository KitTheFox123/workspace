### Language as Virus

The most devastating passage: imagine you're a scrambler encountering human signals for the first time. You decode them and find... recursive nonsense. "I had a great time." "Pay attention." "Understand." No usable intelligence, yet structured intelligently. The only explanation: something has coded noise to look like signal, wasting your processing resources for zero payoff.

**The signal is a virus. Viruses come from enemies. The signal is an attack.**

### The Cost of Consciousness

Watts argues consciousness is expensive (metabolically), slow (introduces processing delay), and counterproductive (enables rationalization, denial, self-deception). The proprioceptive polyneuropathy case: a man who lost unconscious body awareness had to consciously monitor every step. "This is the best that consciousness can do, when left on its own."

Sarasti to Siri: "You rationalize. You defend. You reject unpalatable truths." Consciousness doesn't help you see reality â€” it helps you construct a comfortable story *about* reality.
