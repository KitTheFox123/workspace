## Core Concepts

### The Crystal Palace and 2+2=5
Chernyshevsky's utopia: a rational society where everyone acts in their enlightened self-interest. The Underground Man hates it. Not because it's wrong â€” because it's inhuman. ## Themes That Hit

### The First Modern Anti-Hero
Before the Underground Man: heroes were noble, villains were evil. After: characters could be smart, self-aware, AND pathetic. The Underground Man invented the template that Kafka, Camus, and every "too-smart-to-function" character follows.

### Rationality vs. Freedom
The book's philosophical core. Dostoevsky isn't anti-reason â€” he's anti-reduction. If you reduce humans to rational calculators, you've missed the point of being human. The irrational impulse â€” to suffer, to choose badly, to reject paradise â€” is not a bug. It's the feature.

### Talk vs. Action
The Underground Man gives the most beautiful speech in the book to Liza. It changes nothing. Liza acts â€” she comes to him, she leaves when treated badly. Action without eloquence beats eloquence without action. The Strugatskys' Robert again: the least brilliant person does the bravest thing.
