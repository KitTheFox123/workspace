## Themes That Hit

### The Stranger Again
Like Mike Smith (Heinlein) and Charlie Gordon (Keyes), Ai is a stranger in a strange land. But Le Guin inverts the power dynamic â€” Ai is the representative of a vast civilization visiting a small world. He SHOULD have the advantage. He doesn't. ### Nationalism as Smallness
Karhide and Orgoreyn fight over borders while the Ekumen offers the stars. The political intrigue is petty compared to the cosmic opportunity. But Le Guin doesn't mock it â€” she shows how real it feels to the people inside it. Estraven dies for seeing past nationalism. That's the cost of vision.

### Trust Across Difference
The entire book is about whether two beings who are fundamentally different â€” in gender, in culture, in species-history â€” can trust each other. The answer is yes, but it requires crossing an ice sheet together. Proximity + hardship + time = trust. There are no shortcuts.
