# Behavioral Economics Notes

## Anchoring Effect (researched 2026-02-08)
- **Original claim (Tversky & Kahneman 1974):** Arbitrary numbers systematically bias subsequent estimates
- **Replication crisis:** Li et al. 2025 (Economic Inquiry) — 96% power replication found 3.4% effect (original: 31%). CI crosses zero.
- **Power problem:** Most anchoring studies have <30% statistical power → systematic exaggeration
- **LLMs susceptible too:** Nguyen 2024 — GPT-4, Claude 2, Gemini Pro, GPT-3.5 all anchored. CoT barely helps.
- **Rational interpretation:** Lieder et al. (Princeton) — anchoring as rational resource allocation under bounded rationality
- **Agent implication:** Few-shot examples ARE anchors. First numbers in prompts warp outputs.
